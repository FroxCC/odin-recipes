Broaster Chicken

Description

It is complicated to say about broasted chicken within a sentence. Actually, it is a type of fried chicken. But the main difference from any other fried chicken is that in this cooking method chicken is cooked in a closed frying pan like a pressure cooker called Broasting machine. By this cooking process chicken pieces first marinated in the refrigerator then coated with spices and all-purpose flour and then fried in a Broasting pot. As the Broaster pot work like a pressure cooker that is why some times it is also called pressure cooker fryer or pressure fryer. This recipe was invented by the company Broaster and it is the trademark of Broaster company.

Ingredients

  • Chicken 1 kg (cut it into eight pieces)
  • All-purpose flour 2 cups
  • Turmeric powder ½ tsp
  • Red chili powder ½ tsp
  • food color ¼ tsp
  • One egg white
  • Ginger garlic powder 2 tsp
  • Black pepper powder 1 tsp
  • Buttermilk 1 liter
  • Salt to taste

Steps

    • At first, cut a whole chicken into eight pieces then wash all the pieces very well by freshwater. Drain off all water from the chicken pieces.
    • Now take 1-liter buttermilk in a container and sink all the chicken pieces into it. Close the jar and keep it in a refrigerator for 8 to 12 hours for marination. Remember, marinating time not exceed 24 hours.
    • After complete marination process, remove the chicken pieces from the refrigerator and drain off the buttermilk. Make sure all excess buttermilk drained off perfectly.
    • Now take all the spices (Turmeric powder, red chili powder, food color, ginger-garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and ground all spices) in a bowl and mix them well. Add this mixture on all sides of the chicken pieces.
    • Take another two bowls and add all-purpose flour in one bowl and egg white in another. Dip all the chicken pieces in egg white one by one then coated with all-purpose flour. All sides are coated perfectly by all-purpose flour.
  3. Now take all the pieces in a tray and covered with a plastic paper. Keep the chicken tray in a refrigerator for a minimum of 1 hour but do not exceed 12 hours. Exceeding 12 hours could result in a dark coating when cooked. After this process, remove the chicken pieces from the refrigerator.
  4. Take a Broaster (pressure fryer) and heat cooking oil in it at 360F degree temperature. If Broaster is not available, then use a pressure cooker for frying.
  5. Dip all the chicken pieces one by one in hot cooking oil and then close the pressure cooker. Cook it with medium flame up to 7-8 minutes.
  6. After 8 minutes open the pressure cooker and take one chicken piece out. Check it perfectly cooked or not. If it cooked perfectly then remove all the pieces from the cooker or Broaster.
  7. Keep them on kitchen paper to absorb extra oil. After five minutes, you can serve your favorite broasted chicken.
